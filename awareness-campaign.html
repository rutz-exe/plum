LinkedIn

Strategy

Thought leadership content on company culture and growth, not just an insurance provider. The content will be sharp, insightful, and designed for high engagement, without being overtly sales-y, and will cater more towards startups.

Content

Text-only posts:

Short, provocative posts (150-200 words) that challenge conventional wisdom.

  • "Your ESOPs vest in 4 years. Burnout takes 4 months. Protect your most valuable assets."
  • "You hired a 10x engineer. But their spouse just had a medical emergency, and your basic insurance plan doesn't cover it. Suddenly, their focus isn't on your code. It's on their bills. A great team is a supported team."
  • "Stop calling it a 'perk'. A comprehensive health plan isn't a bonus, it's a business continuity plan. It's the infrastructure that keeps your team running at full capacity."
Insight carousels:

Highly visual carousels with minimal captions. They will use witty comparisons to highlight what teams actually value.

  • "You offer Friday beers. They want to know a root canal won't bankrupt them."

YouTube

Strategy

Develop the Plum YouTube channel into a credible resource for founders on the subject of building world-class teams and culture. Balance in-depth, valuable long-form content with fun, relatable short-form videos that showcase a modern workplace vibe.

Content

Full-Form Videos (8-15 minutes):

These are high-value, evergreen pieces designed to build authority and trust.

  • Example: Analyze successful people-first strategies of well-respected Indian startups (e.g., Zomato, Postman). Interview HR leaders or early employees to discuss specific policies (like their approach to leave, mental health, benefits) that made a real difference.
  • Idea 2: "Ask a VC." A Q&A format where we have a friendly VC answer anonymous questions from founders about what investors really look for in a team, a culture, and a leader.
Shorts (15-45 seconds):

Fun, non-cringey workplace content that feels authentic and relatable to a startup audience. The key is to be observational, not performative.

Meta Ads

The current ad strategy is effective in its copy and creative direction. The plan is to build upon this by introducing a new, high-trust video format and doubling down on precision targeting.

Events

Create memorable, high-value experiences that generate goodwill and position Plum as a core ecosystem partner.

Ex:

An exclusive, invite-only event where two respected founders share their biggest non-product mistakes in hiring, culture, and operations. This creates a space for authentic connection and positions Plum as a brand that understands the real founder journey.

Cafes

Create clever, unexpected brand touchpoints in the daily lives of founders.

Execution:

Partner with popular "work" cafes in startup hubs to distribute beautifully designed coasters with witty, thought-provoking copy.

Examples:

  • "Your competitor isn't just offering a better salary. They're offering better sleep."
  • "Your ESOPs vest in 4 years. Burnout takes 4 months. Protect your assets."
  • "You can't A/B test your team's health. Get it right the first time."
